"corner" may be slightly misleading: it's a whole basement with more than ample seating. hidden is true: i spent a few months walking straight past it before i discovered it.

but that was years ago, back when this space was taken up by the _Muse Coffeehouse_, what was one of my favorite cafes of all time. i've asked, and the two are definitely connected—how, i dunno. please let me know if you discover anything.

muse had solid coffee, but exceptional atmosphere and _exceptional_ chinese baked goods. hidden corner lives up to that. would i cross the city to come here? maybe not. but i make a point of popping in whenever i'm in chinatown. it feels alive and authentic (in the existentialist sense) in a way you just can't find downtown.

if you're coming, _take advantage of the drink + baked good deal._ 2.50 for these muffins is baller.
