Coffee

coffee is—a bean? a drink? a plant? café—a shop? a thin wrapper around caffeine? the first ray of light in a cold, dark morning? a signifier that you're not british? the fuel of the enlightenment, the french revolution? a necessary part of hosting? a little plastic pod, a push of a button?

the phylogeny of coffee is tea: the leaves were steeped and the berries chewed. the yemeni monopoly was broken, and great slave plantations were planted. unfortunately, the ontogeny of coffee is often still slavery and exploitation. the vast majority of coffee is interchangable. the world coffee birthed is fast and smooth.

for the sake of this review site, we're talking about the drink, as served. i can't vouch for how the beans perform at home, but it's generally safe to extrapolate.

Turkish

alternatively known as arabic coffee. spread throughout the middle east by the ottoman empire, it's served with the grounds in the cup, like cupping coffee. for historical reasons, these days, it tends to be served with spices.
