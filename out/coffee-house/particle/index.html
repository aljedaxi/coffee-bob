Particle Coffee

clean washed coffees and fantastic seasonals

Particle is the brainchild of Alex Cao, sole barista of Particle and lead barista at A-OK Cafe in chinook mall. (full disclosure: i'm Alex's biggest fan. i may have a couple biases) fortunately, he does get help some days, but for the most part, you'll be seeing him behind the bar.

good for us; Alex is an exceptional barista with a clear, piercing vision for what he wants his cafe and coffee to be. in a local, regional, and continental scene moving towards more experimental, funky coffees, Alex stocks 95% washed coffees, and 5% restrained naturals. the selection changes monthly, so it's imprudent to speak in concretes, but everything's been expectional so far (—and an exceptional price, considering what he's bringing in.)

on the other hand, we have seasonals. they also lean on clean, elegant profiles: matcha with a coconut water base; oat milk dirty with a gardenia syrup; shakerato with a little yuzu syrup. those're my favorites, but there're always a few approachable options for your coffee curious starbucks drinkers.

Particle's a pop-up, so come next year, it may move. it's currently in house 831, which you may remember as Sociality House. nota bene: Particle is only open on weekends.

pour overs

big on the nose; round in the mouth; elegant on the palate

there's usually a selection of 3-7 different beans; same roaster, different origins, almost all washed.

they're brewed in a zero bypass brewer for maximum cleanliness. everything comes out smooth, elegant, and well balanced. expect a round, juicy brew.

short drinks

3/3
my man makes a mean cortado

you can't go to Particle without getting an oat milk one and one. if you're feeling saucy, consider the 1&1&1: a spro, a cortado, and a dirty or a shakerato.

drinks

3/3
some really neat stuff

i'd roughly group them into two categories: stuff for non-coffee people, and stuff for coffee people.

if you're in the spro zone, there'll be something like the oat milk dirty with a gardenia syrup.

for non coffee people, there's usually something like a caramel macchiato; a couple things with matcha; something with houjicha. they're always great, but i wouldn't let them distract you from the pourover menu.

vibes

2/3
tech worker

not my bag, but your milage will vary. Particle is currently in house 831. on weekdays, house 831 is a members only co-working space; on weekends, the bottom floor opens up, but upstairs is usually cordoned off for something members only. if the front is stuffed, there's probably space in the back.

most surfaces are flat white or light, exposed wood grain. dark green leaves, hanging vines, big open windows. all natural light. the walls are sparcely decorated with photographs of skate parks and oceans.

there's a truly lovely stoop out front. if you want to watch the traffic roll down 17th, there's no better place. for the cloud gazers, the patio in the backyard is gorgeous, usually empty, and dog friendly.
