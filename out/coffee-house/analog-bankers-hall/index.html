Analog — Banker's Hall

Really weirdly beautiful

a plus 15 is a glass box, 15 meters above the ground. you pass through that glass box into a tall, slim glass box. You pass countless, uniform glass boxes, housing countless, multiform businesses. tinted windows chill light; it spills out onto marble.

marble is the odd accent in dark wood. green snakes down the rafters, and faded ruby tiling holds up the espresso machine. dark wood laminates spills out from the machine, bearing low leather seating and wide wooden tables. the lights are round and warm.

does this location have a distinct identity to—say—the Deville in the Google building? no. does it—as the kids say—beat the airspace allegations? certainly not. but the way it suddenly shatters the undifferentiated droll of the +15s is irresistible—every now and then.
